# Fishing Frenzy — Brand Guide

## 1. Overview

Fishing Frenzy is a cozy, pixel-art fishing RPG with a warm, nostalgic aesthetic. The game blends retro charm with modern mobile game conventions — inviting players into a lush, handcrafted world of waterways, cabins, and adventure. The tone is lighthearted, playful, and welcoming.

---

## 2. Art Style

### Pixel Art
- **Resolution:** Mid-resolution pixel art — detailed enough for rich environments but retaining a chunky, handmade feel. Not ultra-low-res retro; not HD-smooth. Think "modern pixel art" with visible pixels at roughly 2–4x native scale.
- **Line work:** Clean outlines with intentional pixel placement. No anti-aliasing — hard pixel edges throughout.
- **Shading:** Uses 3–5 color ramps per material. Dithering is used sparingly for gradients (e.g., water depth transitions). Shading leans warm even in shadows.
- **Animation:** Subtle idle animations — water ripples, character bobbing in boat, foliage sway. Motion is gentle and looping, contributing to the cozy atmosphere.

### Environment
- **Organic, lived-in worlds:** Cabins with weathered wood, mossy rocks, lily pads floating naturally, waterfalls with spray. Environments feel handcrafted and dense with detail.
- **Layered depth:** Foreground elements (lily pads, water surface) → midground (character, boat, rocks) → background (buildings, trees). Parallax-like visual depth even in 2D.
- **Nature-forward:** Water, greenery, and natural textures dominate. Man-made structures (cabins, docks) use natural materials — wood, stone, thatch.

## 3. Color Palette

### Primary Colors
| Role | Hex | Description |
|------|-----|-------------|
| **Water Blue** | `#3B7DD8` | Deep, rich blue — the dominant scene color |
| **Water Highlight** | `#5BA0E8` | Lighter blue for water surface reflections |
| **Water Dark** | `#2A5BA0` | Deeper blue for water depth/shadows |
| **Foliage Green** | `#4CAF50` | Bright, lively green for trees and plants |
| **Dark Foliage** | `#2E7D32` | Deeper green for tree shadows and dense canopy |

### Accent / UI Colors
| Role | Hex | Description |
|------|-----|-------------|
| **Gold / Button** | `#E8A830` | Warm golden-orange — primary button and UI accent color |

| **Button Text** | `#704010` | Dark warm brown for text on gold buttons |

| **Red Accent** | `#D84040` | Alert red — used for notification dots and sale badges |
| **Cream / Light** | `#F0E0C0` | Warm off-white for highlights and light UI areas |

### Character Colors
- Characters use bright, saturated tones that pop against the blue/green environments.
- Skin tones are warm. Hair and accessories use distinct hues for readability at small scale.

### Color Principles
- **Warm over cool:** Even blues lean warm (slightly purple-tinted). Shadows use warm browns/deep blues, never grey or black.
- **High saturation:** Colors are vivid and cheerful, not muted or pastel.
- **Contrast for readability:** UI elements use strong value contrast (dark outlines on gold buttons) to remain legible over busy pixel backgrounds.

## 4. Typography & Text

### In-Game UI Text
- **Style:** Pixel font — chunky, all-caps or mixed-case depending on context. Bold and highly legible at small sizes.
- **Button labels:** ALL CAPS, thick pixel letterforms (e.g., "SHOP", "RANK", "TRAVEL", "Hold to Cast").
- **Weight variation:** Important actions use heavier/larger text ("Hold to Cast" is bolder than "Change Rod").
- **Color:** Dark brown or near-black on golden/cream backgrounds. White with dark outline for text over scene elements.
- **Outline/Shadow:** Text over gameplay areas uses a 1px dark outline or drop shadow for readability.

### Marketing / External Text
- When used outside the game (web, social, print), typography should evoke the same pixel-art warmth:
  - Use pixel-style display fonts for headlines.
  - Body text can use clean sans-serif fonts but should pair with pixel-art decorative elements.
  - Maintain the warm brown + gold color scheme for text.

## 5. UI / Interface Design

### Button Style
- **Shape:** Rounded rectangular pixel buttons with visible 2–3px borders.
- **Color:** Golden-orange (`#E8A830`) with darker border (`#C07820`), creating an embossed/raised look.
- **Text:** Dark brown, ALL CAPS, centered.
- **States:** Pressed state darkens the fill and shifts text down 1–2px (classic pixel button feel).

### Panel / Container Style
- **Background:** Semi-transparent dark panels or warm wood-textured frames.
- **Borders:** Pixel-perfect borders, often using a darker shade of the fill color.
- **Corners:** Slightly rounded in pixel terms (1–2px corner cuts).

### Icon Style
- **Size:** Consistent square icon frames across the sidebar (approx. 48–64px equivalent).
- **Frame:** Each icon sits in a golden/cream bordered square tile.
- **Art:** Simplified pixel illustrations that read clearly at small sizes — silhouette-first design.
- **Notification badges:** Small red circles with white numbers, positioned top-right of icon tiles.

### HUD Layout
- **Left sidebar:** Vertical stack of feature buttons (Rank, Shop, Bag, Pass, Karma).
- **Right sidebar:** Vertical stack of social/event buttons (Daily, Friend, Quest, Sale).
- **Top bar:** Player avatar (top-left), level/currency display (center-top), resource indicators.
- **Bottom area:** Context-sensitive action buttons (Cast, Change Rod, Use Bait, Travel).
- **Philosophy:** Frame the scene — UI hugs the edges, leaving the center for the beautiful game world.

## 6. Tone & Voice

### Personality
- **Cozy & inviting:** The game feels like a relaxing escape. Nothing is stressful or aggressive.
- **Playful & charming:** Lighthearted humor, cute details (bird sitting on the character's head), quirky accessories (sunglasses while fishing).
- **Rewarding:** Players feel a sense of progression and discovery. UI reinforces this with daily rewards, quests, ranks, and passes.

### Writing Style
- Short, punchy labels. One or two words max for buttons.
- Friendly and casual. Never formal or technical.
- Action-oriented: "Hold to Cast", "Use Bait", "Change Rod" — direct and clear.
- Feature names are simple and universal: Shop, Bag, Quest, Travel.

---

## 7. World & Theme

### Setting
- Lush freshwater environments — ponds, rivers, lakes surrounded by forest.
- Rustic, cozy architecture — wooden cabins, docks, stone foundations, waterfalls.
- Seasonal/biome variety implied by the Travel system.

### Character Design
- Chibi-proportioned pixel characters — large heads, expressive faces, small bodies.
- Highly customizable appearance (sunglasses, hats, pets/companions on head).
- Characters ride in wooden rowboats — a core gameplay visual.

### Fauna & Flora
- Lily pads, water flowers, and clover-like ground cover fill the water surface.
- Trees are lush, rounded canopy shapes in multiple green tones.
- Wildlife companions (birds, etc.) add personality.

---

## 8. Brand Don'ts

- **No realistic or HD art.** Everything stays within the pixel-art language.
- **No cold/sterile palettes.** Avoid greys, stark whites, or cool-toned shadows.
- **No placeholder or emoji art.** Every visual element must match the game's handcrafted quality.
- **No aggressive or competitive tone.** This is a chill fishing game, not a battle arena.
- **No cluttered centers.** UI frames the edges; the game world breathes in the middle.
- **No thin or serif fonts in UI.** Keep all in-game text chunky and pixel-native.
